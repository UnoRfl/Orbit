Part I · Terms of Service

01Who we are

Orbit ("Orbit", "we", "us") is a free co-study platform that helps students see when friends are free, share class schedules, and plan study sessions, meals, and hangouts around them. By creating an account or using Orbit, you agree to these Terms and to the Privacy Policy in Part II. If you don't agree, please don't use Orbit.

02Who can use Orbit

You must be at least 13 years old. If you're under 18, you may only use Orbit with the consent of a parent or guardian where the law requires it. Orbit is built for students, but anyone who meets the age requirement is welcome.

03Your account

You can sign up with an email and password, or sign in with Google. Keep your sign-in details to yourself — you're responsible for what happens on your account. Give accurate information, use one account per person, and don't impersonate anyone else. If you think someone accessed your account without permission, change your password and contact us right away.

04Acceptable use

Orbit is a shared space. Don't use it to:

  • Harass, threaten, bully, or discriminate against anyone.
  • Impersonate another person or misrepresent who you are.
  • Post content that is illegal, sexually explicit, or promotes harm.
  • Share another person's schedule, location, or personal details outside Orbit without their consent.
  • Probe, scrape, spam, overload, or attempt to gain unauthorized access to Orbit, its database, or other people's accounts.

Breaking these rules can lead to content removal, suspension, or a permanent ban.

05Your content

Your schedules, profile, systems, planets, events, and messages belong to you. By posting them, you give Orbit permission to store and display them to the audience you chose (for example, your accepted friends or the members of your system) so the app can work. You can edit or delete your content at any time, and deleting it removes it from other people's views.

06Reports & moderation

You can report accounts or content that break these rules. Reports are reviewed by Orbit staff. Staff can temporarily suspend accounts that violate the rules; suspended accounts can't post or interact until the suspension ends. Moderation actions are logged. If you believe an action against your account was a mistake, contact us and we'll take a second look.

07Availability

Orbit is free and under active development. Features may change, break, or be paused while we build. We work to keep Orbit online and your data intact, but the service is provided "as is" without warranties of any kind, and we can't promise uninterrupted or error-free operation.

08Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, Orbit is not liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages arising from your use of the service — including missed meetups, schedule mix-ups, or lost content. Orbit is free, and our total liability for any claim is limited to the amount you paid us to use it: nothing. Nothing in these terms limits liability that cannot be limited under applicable law.

09Ending your account

You can stop using Orbit at any time and ask us to delete your account and data (see section 17). We may suspend or terminate accounts that violate these terms or put other users at risk.

10Changes to these terms

If we make meaningful changes, we'll post the update here and announce it in the app's Updates feed. Continuing to use Orbit after a change takes effect means you accept the new terms.

11Governing law

These terms are governed by the laws of the Republic of the Philippines. If something goes wrong, talk to us first — most problems can be fixed with a message.

Part II · Privacy Policy

12What we collect

  • Account basics — your name, handle, and email.
  • Google sign-in — if you use it, Google shares your name, email address, and profile photo with Orbit. We never see or store your Google password.
  • Profile details you choose to add — course, school, bio, pronouns, hobbies, links, avatar, and cover photo.
  • Schedules — the classes you add or import.
  • Social data — friendships, the systems you join, the planets and events you create, pings, and notifications.
  • Presence — only the campus zone or place you actively choose to share, and only while sharing is switched on.
  • Device tokens — if you enable push notifications, a token that lets us deliver them.

13What we never collect

  • No GPS or background location — presence is a zone you pick, not a tracker.
  • No contact-list uploads.
  • No payment information — Orbit has nothing to buy.
  • No advertising trackers, and we never sell or rent your data to anyone.

14Who can see what

Visibility is enforced by access rules inside the database itself, not just in the app:

  • Your schedule — only friends you've accepted.
  • Your presence — only your friends, only while sharing is on, and never while you're in ghost mode.
  • Systems & planets — only members of that system.
  • Events — only the host and invited people.
  • Reports you file — you and Orbit's support staff.
  • Profile basics (name, handle, course, badges) — visible to signed-in Orbit users so friends can find you.

15How we use your data

Only to run Orbit: showing schedules and free time to your friends, powering the map, delivering pings and notifications you asked for, and keeping the community safe through moderation. We don't use your data for advertising, we don't profile you for third parties, and we don't sell it. Ever.

16Where it lives & security

Orbit's data is stored with Supabase in Sydney, Australia, and is encrypted in transit. Every table is protected by row-level security — the database itself checks who is asking before returning anything — and internal functions run with least privilege. New passwords are screened against known data-breach lists, and account passwords are stored hashed, never in plain text. No online service can promise perfect security, but access to your data is locked down at the lowest layer we control.

17Retention & deletion

We keep your data while your account exists. Notifications delete themselves automatically after 7 days. When you delete content, it disappears from other people's views. To delete your entire account and its data, email us (section 20) from your account's email address — we'll remove your profile, schedule, presence, and social data, and confirm when it's done.

18Your rights

Under the Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173) — and simply because it's your data — you can ask us at any time to show you what we hold about you, correct it, or erase it. Contact us and we'll act on it. If you believe we've mishandled your data, you also have the right to raise a complaint with the National Privacy Commission.

19Children

Orbit is not for children under 13. We don't knowingly collect data from anyone under 13, and we delete such accounts when we find them. If you believe a child under 13 is using Orbit, please tell us.

20Changes & contact

If this policy changes in a meaningful way, we'll update the effective date above and announce it in the app. Questions, deletion requests, appeals, or anything else: improvised30@gmail.com.
